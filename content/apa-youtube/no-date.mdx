# No date

<AnswerCard title="TL;DR" tldr="If a YouTube video does not show an upload date, use (n.d.) to denote 'no date' in both the in-text citation and the reference entry." />

Occasionally a video may not display a clear upload date. In these
cases APA style instructs writers to indicate that no date is
available by writing **(n.d.)**, short for "no date".

<DataBox title="Example with no date">
  <p><strong>In-text citation:</strong> (SciShow, n.d.)</p>
  <p><strong>Reference:</strong> SciShow. (n.d.). Why do cats purr? [Video]. YouTube. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Js3cJQkzS6s</p>
</DataBox>

Including the date on which you accessed the video is optional but
recommended when citing undated material. To do so, add
"Retrieved Month Day, Year, from" before the URL. For example:

<DataBox title="Example with access date">
  <p>SciShow. (n.d.). Why do cats purr? [Video]. YouTube. Retrieved August 30, 2025, from https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Js3cJQkzS6s</p>
</DataBox>
